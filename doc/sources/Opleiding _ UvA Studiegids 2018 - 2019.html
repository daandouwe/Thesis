Mathematics

Programme

Structure of the Programme

Master students of the regular programme choose one of the following four specialisations:

  • Algebra and Geometry;
  • Analysis and Dynamical Systems;
  • Mathematical Physics;
  • Stochastics.

Students can also choose a programme containing one of the following majors and minors:

  • Major Science in Society
  • Major Science Communication
  • Major Teaching
  • Minor Science for Sustainability
  • Minor Tesla
  • Minor Teaching

See for more information about the structure of the Master's programme the Teaching and Examination Regulations -  OER part B

Depending on your specialisation direction you follow in the first year the Master seminar Algebra, Geometry & Mathematical Physics, Analysis & Dynamical Systems, or Stochastics. You compile a programme from the list of constrained choice electives, including a number of basic and advanced courses from the chosen specialisation. The precise requirements are listed in the OER part B. The free electives can be chosen from all master level courses, not necessarily in mathematics.

The second semester of the second year is devoted to the Master project Mathematics. The advisor and subject of the project should be chosen in the first semester of the second year. The master project is carried out under supervision of a staff member of the Korteweg-de Vries Institute. It is possible to combine the master project with an internship. Once a supervisor is found, and the project is discussed with the programme coordinator, the project has to be registered at https://datanose.nl/#project. An independent second reviewer is also needed. The supervisor or the programme coordinator can help with this.

All students compile a personal programme together with the master coordinator, called a personal education plan (PEP). This is subject to the formal approval of the the Examinations Board. Consult the Teaching and Examination Regulations (OER) part B for more information on the programme structure and requirements of the PEP.

Content of the programme

Depending on your specialisation direction, you follow the master seminar Algebra, Geometry and Mathematical Physics, the master seminar Analysis and Dynamical Systems or the master seminar Stochastics. The choice of electives is further constrained per specialisation, as outlined below.

a. Algebra and Geometry

Students take at least three courses from the following list of basic courses:

  • Algebraic Topology
  • Algebraic Geometry 1
  • Algebraic Methods in Combinatorics
  • Algebraic Number Theory
  • Differential Geometry
  • Lie Groups and Lie Algebras
  • Quivers
  • Riemann Surfaces

Students take at least two courses from the list of advanced courses:

  • Advanced Algebraic Geometry: Abelian Varieties
  • Algebraic Geometry 2
  • Algebraic Topology 2
  • Coding and Cryptography
  • Mirror Symmetry
  • Selected Areas in Cryptology
  • Symplectic Geometry
  • Topics in Number Theory
  • Topos Theory

b. Mathematical Physics
Students take at least three courses from the following list:

  • Algebraic Geometry 1
  • Algebraic Topology
  • Differential Geometry
  • Lie Groups and Lie Algebras
  • Riemann Surfaces
  • Quantum Computing

Students take at least one course from the following list:

  • Statistical Physics and Condensed Matter Theory I
  • Quantum Field Theory
  • String Theory

Students take at least two courses from the list of advanced courses:

  • Geometric PDE
  • Mirror Symmetry
  • Operator Algebras
  • Percolation: from Introduction to Frontiers of Current Research
  • Quantum Information Theory
  • Symmetries and Conservation Laws of Nonlinear PDE
  • Symplectic Geometry
  • Topology in Physics

c. Analysis and Dynamical Systems

Students take at least three courses from the following list of basic courses:

  • Differential Geometry
  • Functional Analysis
  • Partial Differential Equations
  • Dynamical Systems
  • Numerical Linear Algebra
  • Numerical Methods for Time-dependent PDE
  • Uncertainty Quantification and Data Assimilation
  • Control of infinite dimensional systems

Students take at least two courses from the list of advanced courses:

  • Advanced Complex Analysis
  • Advanced Topics in Stochastic Analysis
  • Algebraic Topology in Dynamical Systems
  • Geometric Functional Analysis and Its Applications
  • Nonlinear Waves
  • Operator Algebras
  • Symplectic Geometry

d. Stochastics

Measure Theoretic Probability is a compulsory course for this specialization. Students take at least two courses from the following list:

  • Asymptotic Statistics
  • Machine Learning Theory
  • Simulation Methods in Statistics
  • Stochastic Networks
  • Stochastic Processes
  • Stochastic Simulation
  • Data-driven Decision Making in Operations Research

Students take at least two courses from the list of advanced courses:

  • Advanced Topics in Stochastic Analysis
  • Bayesian Statistics
  • Interacting Particle Systems: Theory and Applications
  • Percolation: from Introduction to Frontiers of Current Research
  • Portfolio Theory
  • Queues & Levy Fluctuation Theory
  • Uncertainty Quantification and Data Assimilation
  • Statistical Theory for High- and Infinite-Dimensional Models
  • Interest Rate Models
